About the AI Keyword Generator

Type in one seed keyword and get back a structured keyword research pack — primary keywords, long-tail variations, questions, buying-intent phrases, local and voice-search variants, YouTube titles, blog title ideas, meta suggestions, and a ready-to-use content outline, generated instantly and privately in your browser.

Features

  • 20 keyword and content-idea categories from a single seed keyword
  • Estimated intent, competition, CPC, trend, content score, difficulty, and volume per keyword
  • Filter by difficulty, intent, question keywords, or buying keywords
  • Sort alphabetically, by estimated volume, competition, or keyword length
  • Select individual keywords or grab everything at once
  • Export as TXT, CSV, JSON, or Markdown

How to Use

Enter your main keyword, set your country, industry, and target audience, choose how many ideas you want (100/250/500), then tap Generate keywords. Use the filters and sort options to narrow down the list, select the keywords you want, and export or copy them.

Benefits

  • Nothing is uploaded — all generation and scoring happens locally in your browser
  • No signup, no daily limit, no credit card
  • Consistent, repeatable results — the same seed keyword always produces the same estimated metrics
  • Covers keyword research and content planning (titles, FAQs, outlines) in one tool instead of several

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this real keyword-planner data?
No — this site has no backend or API connection to any paid keyword-research platform (such as Google Keyword Planner), so any tool claiming to show that without one would be misrepresenting its numbers. Instead, this generates keyword ideas through structured template-based expansion and attaches consistent, estimated metrics for ideation and prioritization — a legitimate, widely-used approach for the free tier of many keyword tools, but always verify final keyword choices with your own search console or a dedicated keyword-data tool before relying on the numbers.

Why does the same keyword always get the same estimated volume?
The estimates are deterministically generated from the keyword text itself, not randomized — so your results are consistent and comparable across sessions instead of changing every time you regenerate.

Can I use this for YouTube or local SEO?
Yes — dedicated YouTube Keywords and Local Keywords sections are included alongside the main keyword-research categories.
